Joseph von Eichendorff
        / Das Marmorbild

Das Marmorbild (The Marble Statue) is a novella by Joseph von Eichendorff, a prominent German romantic author. Set in the 19th century, it follows the protagonist, Florio, on a journey filled with mysterious encounters and supernatural elements. Eichendorff’s work often explores themes of longing, nostalgia, and the pursuit of beauty in nature.

The book’s elegant design, featuring a light green linen cover, silk-screened with golden accents, complements the enchanting tale. The typography is carefully chosen to complement the lyrical and poetic style of Joseph von Eichendorff’s writing. As readers turn the pages, they encounter small, dreamy nature elements floating between the text. Overall, the books design captivates the enchanting atmosphere of the novella, reflecting the romantic narrative.

Publication design. Muthesius University | 2022
